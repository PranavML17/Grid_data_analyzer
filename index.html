📚 Key terms explained
Grid & electricity
Grid headroom MOST IMPORTANT
How much spare electricity capacity the local substation has for new connections. A substation at 90% capacity has very little headroom — connecting a high-power charger will be slow or refused. At 55%, plenty of headroom — new connections are fast.
Example: Whitefield 220kV = 57% loaded → HIGH headroom → new 120kW charger approved in 4–6 weeks. Koramangala 66kV = 93% → LOW headroom → 4–6 months + augmentation.
Loading %
Current demand as a percentage of the substation’s maximum capacity. 60% = comfortable. 85%+ = constrained, delays likely. 93%+ = effectively full, new HT connections blocked.
Think of it like a motorway: 60% capacity = traffic flows. 90% = slowdowns. 100% = gridlock.
DISCOM
Distribution Company — the electricity utility operating wires and meters in your area. Sets your tariff and approves/rejects new connection applications.
Bengaluru: BESCOM. Mumbai: MSEDCL. Chennai: TANGEDCO. Hyderabad: TSSPDCL. Delhi: BSES/TPDDL.
HT vs LT connection
LT = Low Tension (below 1kV, up to ~100 kW). HT = High Tension (11kV+, above 100 kW). Large EV hubs need HT. HT takes longer to approve but has lower per-unit tariff.
Demand charge
A fixed monthly fee based on your peak power demand in kW — separate from energy consumed. For large users, 30–50% of total bill. BESS can reduce it by preventing demand spikes.
Peak at 500 kW for 15 minutes? You pay the demand charge on 500 kW for the whole month.
Round-trip efficiency (RTE)
Energy recovered / energy stored × 100. 90% RTE = for every 100 kWh stored, you recover 90 kWh. LFP cells: 92–96%. NMC: 88–93%.
ToD pricing (Time of Day)
Tariff where price changes by time of day. Off-peak (10pm–6am) rates much cheaper than peak (6pm–10pm). Critical for BESS arbitrage: charge cheap at night, save during expensive peak.
EV charging
Utilisation %
% of total available charging time when a vehicle is actually charging. 100% impossible. India average 2025: 15–35% for public stations. Higher = faster payback.
Breakeven utilisation
Minimum utilisation % at which the station covers all costs. Below this = losing money every hour. Above this = profitable.
