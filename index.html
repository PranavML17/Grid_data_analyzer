DISCOM connection — 5 steps
1
Site feasibility check
Visit your local BESCOM sub-division office. Ask for load availability at the nearest 11kV feeder for your required kVA. Determines if a dedicated transformer is needed.
Timeline: 1–3 working days
2
Submit LT-6 / HT-2 application
Apply via bescom.karnataka.gov.in → New Service Connection. Select LT-6(c) for public DC fast charging, HT-2(f) if aggregate demand >100 kW.
Timeline: 7–15 working days
Property docs / leaseSite planLoad schedulePAN / GST
3
BESCOM demand note & payment
BESCOM issues a demand note: security deposit (3 months consumption) + service line charges + transformer contribution if new DT required.
Security deposit: ~₹2–4 lakh for 30 kW station · New DT: ₹4–12 lakh if needed
4
CEA electrical safety compliance
CEA Installation Regulations 2006 + Safety Regulations 2010 apply. BESCOM-licensed wiring supervisor required. Get electrical test certificate before energisation.
Wiring certificateElectrical test reportBIS-certified charger invoice
5
Meter & energisation
BESCOM installs ToD meter and schedules energisation. No separate licence needed to sell electricity for EV charging (MoP order, Sep 2024).
End-to-end: 6–10 weeks (no new DT) · 3–5 months (new DT needed)
PM E-DRIVE — charger infrastructure subsidy
80% subsidy on charger infrastructure cost for public locations. 100% subsidy for government premises (with free public access requirement). BHEL is nodal agency.

Eligibility: Public EVCS, CCS2 or GB/T, registered on MHI portal, minimum 50 kW for highway.

Deadline: Charger deployment window closes March 2026.
MHI PM E-DRIVE notification Oct 2024Verified Jun 2026
Open access rules
No licence required to set up and sell electricity for EV charging (MoP Sep 2024).

Interoperability: All public chargers must accept any EV brand from Jan 2025.

Net metering: Surplus rooftop solar exported to BESCOM at ₹3.68/unit (KERC 2025).
