What is schedule vs actual load?
Every day, BESCOM submits a schedule — a 15-minute block-by-block plan of how much power it will draw from the state grid. The actual is what Bengaluru's consumers really used in real time. The gap is called Unscheduled Interchange (UI). Positive UI = BESCOM drew more than planned = financial penalty.

Active & expiring schemes
PM E-DRIVE Scheme
⚠ Expiring
India's flagship EV incentive. ₹10,900 crore total outlay. ₹3,679 crore for vehicle demand subsidies (e-2W, e-3W, e-buses, trucks, ambulances). ₹2,000 crore for 72,000+ public chargers across cities, highways, airports and ports. 100% subsidy on infrastructure for government premises with free public charger access. BHEL appointed as nodal agency for charger deployment.

ACC PLI Scheme — Battery Cell Manufacturing
● Active
₹18,100 crore PLI for advanced chemistry cell (ACC) battery manufacturing. Target: 50 GWh domestic battery cell production. Beneficiaries include Reliance, Ola Electric, Rajesh Exports. Re-tender opened following Hyundai's exit. Directly reduces EV battery costs for Indian consumers.

Auto & Components PLI
● Active
₹25,938 crore for advanced automotive technology including EV drivetrains, motors, power electronics and BMS. Incentivises domestic manufacturing of components India currently imports — critical for cutting EV costs long-term.

MoP EV Charging Guidelines 2024
Enacted
Ministry of Power mandatory standards for all public charging stations. CCS2 connector required for 4W fast charging (50–500 kW). Type 2 AC for AC charging. 120 kW minimum fast-charging guns for heavy vehicles. Open-access provisions: any EV must be able to charge at any station regardless of OEM. Simplified licensing — no separate licence needed to set up a charging station.
MoPCCS2 mandateOpen accessStandards
Effective September 2024 · Revised Jan 2025 for battery swapping
Battery Swapping & BCS Guidelines
Enacted
MoP released guidelines (Jan 2025) for Battery Swapping Stations (BSS) and Battery Charging Stations (BCS). Promotes Battery-as-a-Service model — separating battery from vehicle reduces upfront EV cost. Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G) applications permitted to aid grid stability. Liquid-cooled swappable batteries encouraged for buses and trucks. Operators may use existing electricity connections without new infrastructure.

Upcoming & proposed
FAME III — Diesel Bus Replacement
◎ Proposed
Under deliberation as successor to FAME II and PM E-DRIVE. Scope shifts entirely to commercial and public transport — target of replacing 800,000 diesel buses with electric alternatives over 7 years. Private car and 2W subsidies will NOT be included (those continue via PM E-DRIVE). Timing tied to state fleet procurement pipelines.
MHIElectric buses800k buses7-year plan
Expected announcement: FY 2026-27
Union Budget 2026-27 — EV Manufacturing
◎ In Effect
Budget removes Basic Customs Duty on Li-ion battery cell machinery and critical minerals processing equipment — reduces capex for domestic gigafactories. Significant for BESCOM's EV tariff cost since cheaper domestic batteries = lower EV cost = higher adoption = more LT-6 revenue for BESCOM.
Finance MinistryBCD removalLi-ion machineryCritical minerals
Effective FY 2026-27
PM Surya Ghar — Rooftop Solar for EV Charging
● Active
Up to ₹78,000 subsidy for rooftop solar installations in Karnataka under this central scheme. Directly relevant to EV charging — rooftop solar + home charger = near-zero cost per km. BESCOM offers net metering: surplus solar exported to grid. Combined with BESCOM's ₹6.00/unit EV tariff, solar-charged EVs are the cheapest transport option in Bengaluru.

Ongoing — apply via pmsuryaghar.gov.in
India Electric Mobility Index (NITI Aayog)
● Live tracker
Launched August 2025 — measures state performance across vehicle electrification, charging readiness, and R&D. Delhi, Maharashtra, and Chandigarh are current leaders. Karnataka ranks high on charging infrastructure. Index will drive competitive state policy-making — states with low scores are expected to announce catch-up policies in FY 2026-27.

100% Domestic Content Requirement — PM E-DRIVE
Enacted 2025
MHI mandated 100% DCR for 18 critical EV components — motors, controllers, BMS, charger modules — for 2W, 3W and e-buses under PM E-DRIVE to qualify for subsidies. Forces OEMs to localise supply chains. Eliminates Chinese component dependency for subsidy-eligible vehicles. Short-term cost pressure; long-term supply chain resilience.

Effective 2025 for all PM E-DRIVE eligible vehicles
