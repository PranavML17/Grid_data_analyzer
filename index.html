DISCOM connection — step by step
Actual process for setting up a public EV charging station under BESCOM jurisdiction. Not a link to a PDF — each step translated.
Application & approval
1
Site feasibility check
Visit or call your local BESCOM sub-division office. Ask for load availability at the nearest 11kV feeder for your required demand (in kVA). This determines if the site can get the power you need without a dedicated transformer.
Timeline: 1–3 working days
2
Submit LT-6 / HT-2 application online
Apply via BESCOM's online portal (bescom.karnataka.gov.in → New Service Connection). Select category: LT-6(c) for public fast charging, HT-2(f) if aggregate demand exceeds 100 kW.
Timeline: Application processing 7–15 working days
Property documents / lease deed Site plan Load schedule PAN / GST Authorisation letter if not owner
3
BESCOM demand note & payment
BESCOM issues a demand note specifying security deposit (typically 3 months estimated consumption), service line charges, and transformer contribution if a new DT is needed. Pay within the validity period (usually 30 days).
Security deposit: ~₹2–4 lakh for 30 kW station · New DT: ₹4–12 lakh if required
4
CEA electrical safety compliance
Your charger installation must comply with CEA (Installation & Operation of Meters) Regulations 2006 and CEA (Measures Relating to Safety & Electric Supply) Regulations 2010. The wiring contractor must be a BESCOM-licensed supervisor. Get an electrical installation test report before energisation.
Wiring completion certificate Electrical test report BIS-certified charger invoice
5
Meter installation & energisation
BESCOM installs a ToD (Time-of-Day) meter. Energisation is scheduled — typically within 7 working days of clearance. You can now operate commercially. No separate licence is needed to sell electricity for EV charging (MoP order, Sep 2024).
End-to-end typical timeline: 6–10 weeks if no new transformer required · 3–5 months if new DT needed
PM E-DRIVE subsidy — EV charging
Under PM E-DRIVE (October 2024), 80% of charger infrastructure cost is subsidised for public locations. Government premises get 100% subsidy with mandatory free public access during non-peak hours.
Eligibility: Public EV charging station, CCS2 or GB/T connector, registered with MHI portal, minimum 50 kW fast charger for highway locations.

How to apply: Through BHEL (nodal agency). Apply via MHI EV portal. Subsidy disbursed post-installation verification.

Deadline: Charger deployment window closes March 2026.
MHI PM E-DRIVE notification, Oct 2024
Verified Jun 2026
Open access rules
No licence required to set up and sell electricity for EV charging (MoP order, September 2024). Any registered business can operate a public charging station.

Network interoperability: From Jan 2025, all public chargers must accept any EV regardless of OEM brand — no proprietary lock-in allowed.

Net metering: If your station has rooftop solar, surplus can be exported to BESCOM grid at ₹3.68/unit (KERC 2025 net metering rate).

Core PLI tranche. Requires 100% DCR on 18 specified components from 2025. Incentive paid for 5 years post-commissioning on actual sales.

Enhanced incentive for vertically integrated operations (mining to cell). Critical minerals sourcing via India's bilateral agreements may qualify for additional support.

100% Domestic Content Requirement — 2025
To receive PLI incentives under ACC or PM E-DRIVE, the following components must be India-manufactured:

Battery cells, BMS, pack housing, cell module connectors, thermal management components, safety devices, cell grading & formation equipment outputs, separator (by 2027), electrolyte (by 2028), cathode active material (by 2028).

Non-compliance on any DCR component = zero incentive for that quarterly production batch.
